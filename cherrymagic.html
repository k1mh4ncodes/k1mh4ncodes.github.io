Cherry Magic

Summary

Adachi Kiyoshi is a regular salaryman who, at the age of 29, was still a virgin. On his 30th birthday, he suddenly finds himself acquiring a new power - the ability to read other people's minds whenever he touches them. Shocked and unnerved by this new ability, he struggles to cope.

That is, until one day he accidentally touches the handsome and popular fellow office-worker, Kurosawa Yuichi, only to find out that Kurosawa actually has secret romantic feelings for him. How will Adachi cope with this new revelation?

(Source: MyDramaList)

Adapted from the manga "30-sai made Dotei Da to Mahotsukai ni Nareru rashii" (30歳まで童貞だと魔法使いになれるらしい) by Toyota Yuu (豊田悠).

Review

Storyline

The storyline is really cute and simple, but out of this world too. The concept sounds really boring but it managed to make all 12 episodes really enjoyable.

Characterization:

Kurosawa’s character is a really respectful person 10/10. He respects Adachi's feelings and I think it's really nice. Adachi’s character is extremely shy and socially awkward and he has managed to make that. I really like fujisaki-san’s character. There is not much aroace representation in the media especially in japanese media.

actors/acting:

The actor for Adachi is really good at portraying his character. Unlike most japanese dramas, he managed to do exaggerated acting really well and really natural.

Cinematography and setting

I really like the soft and ambient lighting of most Japanese dramas, and cherry magic is no different. The scene at Kurosawa’s house is really pretty. The interior design and the use of brown colours with a touch of greens in the background really makes the scene pop. The use of colour makes the characters blend into the scene perfectly. It actually feels like the characters are living in the set.
